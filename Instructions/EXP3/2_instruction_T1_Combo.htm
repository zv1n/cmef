After you are told whether the word pair is related/unrelated and in large/small
font, you will be asked to estimate how likely you will be to learn and remember the word
pair on a later recall test both BEFORE and AFTER you study the word pair.

For example, you may be told that the upcoming word pair is related and in small font.
You would then be asked to type ANY NUMBER between 0 and 100 to indicate how confident
you are that you will be able to learn and recall the word pair on the recall task.
You will then be shown the word pair such as "table - chair" and given 5 seconds to study it.
After the 5 seconds have elapsed you will be asked to provide another judgment using the same
0 to 100 scale to indicate your confidence level.

After providing your judgment, you would be told about the next word pair and whether it is
related/unrelated and in large/small font. You would then provide your prestudy judgment,
study the word pair, and provide your poststudy judgment. This process will repeat until you
have studied all 60 word pairs in Trial 1. You will then begin the recall test where you will
be shown the first word of each pair, "table - ?" and asked to type the second word of the pair,
"chair." Once you have attempted to recall all 60 pairs, you will begin a final
trial that uses different word pairs, but follows the same steps as Trial 1.

If you have any questions at this time, please raise your hand quietly to alert the research
assistant. If you are ready to begin then please click on the word "continue" at the bottom of the
screen.
