In this task you will be asked to try to remember 60 different words presented in
each of 2 study-test trials. Some words represent items typically thought of as loud
(e.g., weedeater), while other words represent items typically thought of as quiet
(e.g., slippers). Other items are neutral with regard to sound. That is they do not
have any sound qualities associated with them (e.g., thermometer).

Each word will be presented for 5 seconds on the screen, during which time you should
try to memorize it. After you study each word you will be asked to predict how likely you
think it is that you will be able to remember that word on the upcoming recall test.
You will provide these judgments using any number between 0-100, where 0 = no confidence
and 100 = complete confidence you'll be able to recall a particular word.

Once you have studied and provided judgments for each of the 60 items, you will be asked
to type all the words you can remember during the recall test. You will then complete a
second study-test trial in which you will study a second set of 60 words.

To advance to the next screen, please click on the word "continue" at the bottom of the
screen; however, be sure to read the instruction screens thoroughly before continuing
because you will be unable to go back.
